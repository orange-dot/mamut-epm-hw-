# Vision: Experimental Performance Monster

## Internal EPM Framing

This vision is shared across `Mamut EPM`.

- `EPM1` is the current software realization in `mamut-sint-sw`
- `EPM2` is the hardware continuation in `mamut-sint-hw`

The behavior target below is therefore meant to survive the line split, not to
belong only to the hardware repo.

## One-Line Definition

`Mamut EPM` should feel like a living performance machine: huge, unstable in a
useful way, immediate under the hands, and capable of moving from beauty to
near-collapse without turning into nonsense.

## What It Is

This is not:

- a vintage clone
- a generic modern poly
- a noise box pretending to be an instrument

This is:

- a playable synth
- with dangerous range
- with strong macro behavior
- with analog weight
- with digital intelligence

## Core Formula

Working formula:

- analog core
- digital nervous system
- 6 voices
- 2 oscillators plus sub per voice
- one strong filter identity
- drive or feedback in the voice or output path
- tonal stack: `Horizont + Pec` as the base, `Baklja` as the rupture layer
- `Gravitacija` as a first-class macro and system behavior
- scene morphing or equivalent large movement control
- presets with memory of personality, not just values

## Desired Feel

The synth should be able to do all of these convincingly:

- immense pads that feel expensive and unstable
- animated chords that sound like the voices are alive
- brutal sync and crossmod gestures
- performance transitions that feel physical
- sounds that bloom, tear, swarm, lean, and overheat

The important thing is contrast. It should be able to sound beautiful and threatening without switching to a different instrument identity.

## Tonal Stack

The core tonal center is now defined as:

- `Horizont`
- plus `Pec`
- with `Baklja` opening through performance controls

### Horizont

This is the large, distant, gravitational side of the synth.

It implies:

- wide, breathing pads
- a sense of air and scale
- depth without softness
- dark grandeur without polite nostalgia

### Pec

This is the dense, heated, low-mid body of the synth.

It implies:

- pressure
- harmonic thickness
- low-mid authority
- internal heat and drive

### Baklja

This is not the base tone.

It is the controlled eruption in the instrument that opens through performance.

It implies:

- instability on purpose
- ripping sync-first gestures with crossmod poison
- edge, damage, mutation, and near-collapse
- a way to push the instrument past beautiful into dangerous

## Behavioral Pillars

### 1. Playable danger

The synth should have access to unstable or extreme behavior, but it should stay musically steerable.

### 2. Big movement

One gesture should be able to move the sound far. This instrument should not require turning ten tiny knobs just to wake up.

### 3. Voice individuality

Each voice can have a little selfhood. Not random failure. Not bad tuning. Individuality.

### 4. Directness

It should be possible to understand and perform the synth from the panel, not only from a hidden menu model.

### 5. Gravitacija and release

The instrument should have a sense of internal tension. Some controls should make it feel like the sound is pushing against its own limits.

## Gravitacija Axis

`Gravitacija` is now the leading candidate for the main end-to-end sound axis beyond the core synth controls.

This is explicitly not a Leslie or rotary-motion concept.

It should behave more like total internal pressure inside the instrument:

- at low values: open, large, expensive, spacious, high headroom
- at medium values: denser, warmer, more loaded, more low-mid body
- at high values: compressed, saturated, harmonically bent, near-overloaded
- at extreme values: close to `Baklja`, but still playable

The point is to move the whole organism of the synth, not just add an effect.

## Performance Layer

The monster identity likely needs a named set of high-level controls.

Current working candidates:

- `Bloom`
  Makes the sound larger, wider, more open, more airborne.

- `Ruin`
  Adds damage, instability, drift, crossmod pressure, and edge.

- `Swarm`
  Makes voices move together as a pack: spread, detune, density, collective motion.

- `Tilt`
  Rebalances the whole patch toward darker/brighter or tighter/looser behavior.

- `Heat`
  Adds saturation, internal pressure, density, and near-overload intensity.

These names are placeholders for now, but the concept matters: the synth needs controls that operate at the level of behavior, not just single parameters.

At the current state of the concept, `Gravitacija` is likely to become either:

- one of the monster controls
- or a separate higher-order axis that the monster controls can push into

## Sound Design Philosophy

The synth should not be tuned only for correctness.

It should be tuned for:

- memory
- surprise
- physical feel
- weight
- motion

If a circuit choice or control behavior makes the instrument more unforgettable, that matters.

The guiding tonal trajectory is now:

- beauty from `Horizont`
- mass and heat from `Pec`
- danger from `Baklja`
- global organism stress through `Gravitacija`

## Role Of The PC4

The `Kurzweil PC4` gives us an immediate performance front end.

That means:

- no waiting on an internal keybed
- immediate velocity and aftertouch testing
- immediate MIDI integration
- the freedom to make the module itself dense, strange, and performance-oriented

## What Must Be Protected

As the project evolves, these things must survive:

- the synth must stay playable
- the synth must stay specific
- the synth must avoid becoming a generic analog poly with a few extra tricks
- the synth must not lose its large-gesture performance identity

## Next Design Moves

The next design pass should define:

1. the exact behavior of `Gravitacija`
2. the oscillator behavior and instability model
3. the first 3 to 5 monster controls
4. how scenes, morphing, or macro states work in performance
