# Baklja Rupture Strategy

## Purpose

This document locks the current rupture direction of `Baklja`.

The key decision is:

- `Baklja` is `sync-first`
- but `crossmod-poisoned`

That means rupture begins from a readable, pitch-centered sync backbone, then becomes stranger and more dangerous as crossmod, asymmetry, and final-stage strain enter the picture.

## Why This Direction

`Baklja` needs to do two things at once:

- stay musically playable in polyphony
- still become genuinely violent when pushed

If rupture is too sync-centric, it can become sharp but too clean.

If rupture is too crossmod-centric, it can become fascinating but lose harmonic dignity too early, especially in dense chords.

This direction is the chosen middle path:

- sync provides the spine
- crossmod provides the poison

## Core Principle

The working rule is:

- `osc1` keeps the musical center
- `osc2` carries the rupture tension
- `sub` protects `Pec`

This is critical because `Baklja` should not dissolve the instrument's body while erupting.

## Oscillator Roles In Baklja

### Oscillator 1

Primary role:

- master
- pitch anchor
- identity stabilizer

In rupture behavior:

- acts as the reference center
- preserves note readability
- prevents the patch from dissolving into vague metallic chaos

`osc1` should stay musically trustworthy even when the patch is under extreme pressure.

### Oscillator 2

Primary role:

- slave
- tension generator
- cut and tearing source

In rupture behavior:

- receives sync pressure
- introduces shape violence
- acts as the first carrier of crossmod poison

`osc2` is where the instrument first starts to split open.

### Sub

Primary role:

- mass
- body
- furnace reinforcement

In rupture behavior:

- should mostly remain loyal to `Pec`
- should not fully participate in rupture unless patch-specific design says otherwise

This matters because the sub keeps the synth heavy while the upper structure tears.

## Chosen Rupture Language

The chosen rupture language is:

- sync-led articulation
- crossmod contamination
- output-stage strain finishing the event

This means the synth should rupture in layers:

1. the oscillator relationship becomes tense
2. sync starts cutting the harmonic shape
3. crossmod introduces inner poison and instability
4. filter and output stages amplify the danger

That progression gives `Baklja` shape rather than randomness.

## Gravitacija Zone Mapping

### Low Gravitacija

Expected rupture state:

- nearly closed

Behavior:

- sync path mostly dormant
- crossmod contamination effectively absent
- oscillator relationship wide and playable

Meaning:

- `Horizont` dominates

### Mid Gravitacija

Expected rupture state:

- armed but not open

Behavior:

- sync becomes available as pressure potential
- oscillator relationship tightens
- crossmod remains mostly hidden
- the player starts to feel edge readiness

Meaning:

- `Pec` is building pressure

### High Gravitacija

Expected rupture state:

- active

Behavior:

- sync becomes clearly audible
- slave oscillator starts tearing under pressure
- crossmod begins to bleed into the tone
- filter strain becomes musically obvious

Meaning:

- this is the true threshold of `Baklja`

### Extreme Gravitacija

Expected rupture state:

- fully opened

Behavior:

- sync violence is explicit
- crossmod poison becomes audible and dangerous
- asymmetry rises
- output stage sounds stressed
- the patch feels near rupture but still steerable

Meaning:

- `Baklja` is open, but the instrument still has a spine

## Rupture Topologies

The current conceptual topologies are:

### 1. Rez

Primary engine:

- sync-heavy

Character:

- cutting
- articulate
- blade-like
- aggressive but still readable

### 2. Plazma

Primary engine:

- crossmod-heavy

Character:

- alien
- unstable
- boiling
- less predictable

Risk:

- can overtake the synth too early

### 3. Rascep

Primary engine:

- sync backbone with crossmod bleed

Character:

- torn
- intelligent
- violent
- unstable in a controlled way

This should be the core `Mamut` rupture identity.

## Where Baklja Should Happen In The Signal Path

`Baklja` should not live in only one place.

It should unfold in three main layers:

### 1. Pre-Filter Rupture

Main candidates:

- sync
- crossmod
- shape conflict

Role:

- generate the core rupture harmonics

### 2. Filter Strain

Main candidates:

- input overload
- nonlinear filter response
- strain near resonance edge

Role:

- translate oscillator violence into a more physical scream

### 3. Final Stage Rascep

Main candidates:

- asymmetry
- bus stress
- output saturation
- headroom reduction

Role:

- make rupture feel like a total instrument event, not just an oscillator trick

## Recommended V1 Distribution

For the first implementation:

- primary rupture source should be pre-filter sync behavior
- secondary poison source should be crossmod bleed
- tertiary finishing layer should be final-stage asymmetry and strain

This keeps the architecture readable while still giving `Baklja` depth.

## What Should Be Patch-Variable

Patches should be able to vary:

- how early sync becomes audible
- how much crossmod poison enters
- how much final-stage strain is allowed
- whether the patch leans toward `Rez`, `Plazma`, or `Rascep`

## What Must Stay Constant

Across patches, the following should remain true:

- `osc1` is still the center
- `osc2` is still the rupture carrier
- `sub` still protects `Pec`
- sync still remains the spine of `Baklja`

## What Baklja Must Not Become

It must not become:

- full-time harshness
- random failure
- crossmod soup
- an oscillator-only gimmick
- a thing that destroys polyphonic confidence

If it cannot hold a chord while violent, it is the wrong rupture strategy for this synth.

## V1 Recommendation

The current V1 rupture recommendation is:

- `osc1` as master and pitch anchor
- `osc2` as sync-slave rupture carrier
- `sub` as `Pec` anchor
- sync as primary `Baklja` language
- crossmod as secondary toxic layer
- final-stage strain as event completion

Short version:

- `Baklja` should be sync-first, crossmod-poisoned

That is the chosen direction.
